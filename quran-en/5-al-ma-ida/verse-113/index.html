    Transliteration:

    Qaaloo nureedu an naakula minhaa wa tatam'inna quloo bunaa wa na'lama an qad sadaqtana wa nakoona 'alaihaa minash shaahideen (QS. al-Māʾidah:113)

    English / Sahih Translation:

    They said, "We wish to eat from it and let our hearts be reassured and know that you have been truthful to us and be among its witnesses." (QS. Al-Ma'idah, ayah 113)

    Mufti Taqi Usmani

    They said, “We wish that we eat from it, and our hearts be satisfied, and we may know for sure that you have told us the truth, and we may become witnesses to it.”

    Dr. Mustafa Khattab, the Clear Quran

    They said, “We ˹only˺ wish to eat from it to reassure our hearts, to verify you are indeed truthful to us, and to become its witnesses.”

    Ruwwad Translation Center

    They said, “We wish to eat from it and let our hearts be reassured and to know that you told us the truth, and to be witnesses to it.”

    A. J. Arberry

    Abdul Majid Daryabadi

    Abdullah Yusuf Ali

    Tafsir jalalayn

    They said, `We desire, to request this in order, to eat of it and that our hearts be reassured, through increased certainty, and that we may know, that we may acquire more awareness [of the fact], that you (annaka is softened to an) have spoken truthfully to us, in your claim to prophethood, and that we may be among the witnesses thereof'.

    Tafseer Ibn Kathir

    قَالُواْ نُرِيدُ أَن نَّأْكُلَ مِنْهَا

    They said;"We wish to eat thereof."

    we need to eat from it,

    وَتَطْمَيِنَّ قُلُوبُنَا

    and to be stronger in faith,

    when we witness it descending from heaven as sustenance for us.

    وَنَعْلَمَ أَن قَدْ صَدَقْتَنَا

    and to know that you have indeed told us the truth,

    of your Message and our faith in you increases and also our knowledge.

    وَنَكُونَ عَلَيْهَا مِنَ الشَّاهِدِينَ



    and that we ourselves be its witnesses.

    testifying that it is a sign from Allah, as proof and evidence that you are a Prophet, and attesting to the truth of what you brought us
