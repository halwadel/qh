    Transliteration:

    Wa yaa qawmi haazihee naaqatul laahi lakum aayatan fazaroohaa taakul feee ardil laahi wa laa tamassoohaa bisooo'in fa yaakhuzakum azaabun qareeb (QS. Hūd:64)

    English / Sahih Translation:

    And O my people, this is the she-camel of Allah – [she is] to you a sign. So let her feed upon Allah's earth and do not touch her with harm, or you will be taken by an impending punishment." (QS. Hud, ayah 64)

    Tafsir jalalayn

    And, O my people, this is the she-camel of God, a sign for you (yatan is a circumstantial qualifier operated by the demonstrative noun [hdhihi, `this']). Leave her to eat in God's earth and do not cause her any harm, [by] hamstringing [her], lest you be seized by a near chastisement', if you do hamstring her.

    Tafseer Ibn Kathir

    Salih said;

    وَيَا قَوْمِ هَـذِهِ نَاقَةُ اللّهِ لَكُمْ ايَةً فَذَرُوهَا تَأْكُلْ فِي أَرْضِ اللّهِ

    وَلَا تَمَسُّوهَا بِسُوءٍ فَيَأْخُذَكُمْ عَذَابٌ قَرِيبٌ
